MINORS COMPULSION TOWARDS DIPSOMANIA

Drug addiction has become a weighty hitch all over the world. The number of teens and youth addicted to drugs is increasing gradually. Drugs are chemical substances which have a sequel on our body. There are two types of drugs, one which is licit and the other which is illicit. Licit drugs are basically the medicines which we use in our daily use for various problems in our body such as body aches, headaches, etc. These drugs are to cure our body ailments.

But the illicit drugs are the ones that are used to get into the state of being inebriated or drunk. Various types of drugs like cocaine, marijuana, heroine, etc, are consumed by the addicts globally. This helps people to lose their senses and forget everything. Consumption of such drugs are illegitimate and can have an adverse effect on our body. It can affect us badly both physically and mentally.

Youngsters often start with just tasting these drugs and then end up getting obsessed to it. The drug addicts becomes dependent on drug that they cannot stops its usage. They do this due to numerous reasons. They consume sometimes because of unfair expectations and stress. The drugs make them feel high and its after-effect are quite relaxing. But it effects, in the long run, can damage their brain, liver, etc.

Students also get into this addiction when they fail to get emotional support. So they turn themselves towards drugs to calm their distress and vexation.

Poverty is also one of the cause behind this drug-selling network. Drug sellers prey on students and make them addicted to drugs. Initially, they give drugs to them unconfined and once they get addicted they start charging them. They also target poor children and make them sell drugs in order to earn some money

Drug consumption makes students mentally and physically unstable. It also attracts various diseases. It makes students depressed and makes their recovery difficult.

There are various therapeutics available for addicts to get free from their addiction towards drugs.
